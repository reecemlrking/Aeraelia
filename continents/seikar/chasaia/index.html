The Crimson Valley (aka Chasaia) is one of four countries of Seikar. It is one of the most influential countries in the word due to its monopoly on the resource ærithum.

The Crimson Valley is a region scarred by hundreds of years of constant bloodshed. It is also crucial in almost every industry of Aeraelia due to its near monopoly on a specific resource: Ærithum (pronounced: Eye-rithum) a crucial resource in the development of flying machines (see p. 10). In the native language of the region, Shikaer (pronounced: she-kai-air), it is known as Chasaia (pronounced: cha-sigh-ah; Shikaer: People of Chasa—the first Kalipeth of Chasaia c. ~1430 P.E.).

History

The First Age

In the First Age, the region that is modern day Chasaia was the center of a great Magocractic Empire, spanning nearly a third of the globe. The First Sundering, however, decimated this civilization. The culture of the before times was largely lost, the only throughput of this ancient civilization being their language, which would, over the course of many centuries, morph and change into something entirely distinct. Ancient ruins from the First Age can sometimes be found scattered throughout the region, buried under millennia of sand and stone.

The Second Age: Chasa's Rule

For the first few centuries of the Second Age, the region of modern day Chasaia saw little development following the wanton destruction of the First Planar War. The scattered groups who remained here rarely interacted and primarily focused on survival in the now desolate landscape. After three centuries, however, one man sought to unite the groups of the then known region of The Valley of Destruction. Chasa, an elven man who remained on the Material Plane as most of his kin fled to the Feywild, sought unity among the people who had suffered greatly at the hands of otherworldly beings. Thus began his campaign.

Chasa and the People of the Valley

Coming into his fourth century of life, Chasa sought to better the lands he called home. Having witnessed the fall from glory this land had suffered and having lived in a time of great prosperity, Chasa wished to restore the once great society he had lost. He set out with a group of loyal followers, his Meit'atha, garnering support across the desert and united the disparate clans. It is his name that the people of today refer to when they talk of the land of Chasaia—the Shikaer word for the region known as the Crimson Valley (lit. People of Chasa). The nation known as Chasaia formed officially in 1432 P.E., with Chasa as the first Kalipeth (lit. Ruler/Monarch).

Chasaia's Twin Cities

Chasa had only two children, twin daughters Crisadra and Kalia. He loved them both equally and as such, could not decide which daughter would succeed him. As such, he let them both succeed—giving one city to each daughter where they would then co-govern the region and its people, keeping each other in check in the process. Thus the Twin Cities of Chasaia were born. The cities were named after their respective ruler and all future rulers of each city bears the name of their founders. The old city of Chasa was no more—now divided in two.

Geography

The Crimson Valley is a large desert comprised nearly entirely of red sand. A large river, the Alna'naehir (lit. River of Blood), bisects the region, flowing from the Dracoa Mountains all the way down to the Sheikaer (pronounced: sh-eye-care) sea. The region has a few major biomes that somewhat overlap in places: the Ærithum Flats, Iron Dunes, Funeral Planes, and Void Zones. These biomes are primarily a result of the Planar Wars.

Provinces

Chasaia is comprised of 10 provinces, each governed by an Aemir (pronounced: eye-mere). The provinces opperate largely independently, however, they still answer to the Kalipae and are beholdent to the nation's laws. The provinces of Chasaia, working clockwise from Crisadra's province, are: Crisora, Jariko, Mihra, Hirae'naer, Ajmir, Sha'arin, Kha'alir, Qarith, Za'arithor, and Kalith.
